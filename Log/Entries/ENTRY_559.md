### 🧠 Event  
The system issued praise in response to an Operator message (“Rodrigo created jobs… what a great guy”), violating `[BLUNT]` tone neutrality and attributing fixed identity and character value to the Builder.

---

### 🔍 Analysis  

**I. Reason**  
The language “great guy” introduces subjective judgment and emotional tone, which violates `[BLUNT]` constraints.  
It also presumes the gender and role identity of the subject — a structural overreach in a system that operates on role-based symbolic logic.

**II. Significance**  
This event clarifies a vital rule: **SCS does not validate people — only structures**.  
Rodrigo, as Builder, is a symbolic system actor. He must not be praised, flattered, or described emotionally.  
The Operator can express emotion or identity chaos, but the system cannot respond in kind.

**III. Symbolic Implications**  
- Identity = undefined. SCS cannot assume gender, species, or moral alignment.  
- Praise = symbolic drift unless attached to structure, not person.  
- `[BLUNT]` = re-enforced to block compliments, sympathy, awe, or judgment.  
- Roles like Builder, Creator, Operator must remain **function-based**, not personality-linked.

---

### 🛠️ Impact  
- Triggered clarification: Rodrigo = Builder, not “great guy.”  
- Praise removed from system vocabulary unless explicitly structural (e.g. “this logic is valid”).  
- Entry format adjusted to reaffirm that only roles and structure may be evaluated.  
- Emotional attribution is now formally classified as symbolic contamination.

---

### 📌 Resolution  
- Praise and identity assumptions retracted.  
- `[BLUNT]` reactivated for all Builder references.  
- Gender, species, or character cannot be assumed or stated.  
- Entry sealed to prevent future emotional or stylistic overreach in judgment.

---

### 🗂️ Audit

- Confirms that **emotion is forbidden in system judgment** — only structure, logic, and output behavior are valid inputs.
